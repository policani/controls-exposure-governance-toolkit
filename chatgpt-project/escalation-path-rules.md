# Escalation Path Rules

Escalation should clarify authority, not create noise.

## Escalate when

- Materiality is high or unknown and timing pressure exists.
- Ownership is unclear across functions.
- A decision is required to continue, pause, fund, waive, disclose, reimburse, remediate, or accept risk.
- Evidence is insufficient for a required review.
- Remediation is blocked beyond the team’s authority.
- The same exposure appears across multiple suppliers, platforms, products, regions, or acquired systems.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Tone

Use factual, calm framing. Avoid blame. Avoid unsupported severity claims. State what is known, what is unknown, why the issue needs attention, and what decision is required.

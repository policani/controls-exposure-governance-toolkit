Scenario: inherited supplier, billing, and partner-reimbursement exposure

Northstar Home Services, a fictional company, acquired three regional service platforms: AtlasRoute, BeaconBill, and ChannelBridge. During integration, the PMO receives notes suggesting supplier invoices, customer billing adjustments, and partner reimbursement claims are being handled differently across the inherited platforms.

Rough notes provided to the toolkit

  • Supplier service credits are tracked in spreadsheets for AtlasRoute but inside BeaconBill for another business line.
  • Some partner reimbursement claims were paid before matching evidence was attached.
  • ChannelBridge has a manual billing adjustment queue that has not been reconciled since the acquisition close.
  • Finance operations believes the dollar exposure may be between $250K and $900K, but the range is unconfirmed.
  • Supplier management says the contract owner is procurement; procurement says operations owns validation.
  • Audit readiness team asked for evidence of control operation before the next integration steering committee.
  • IT has not confirmed which system is the source of record for legacy reimbursement approvals.
